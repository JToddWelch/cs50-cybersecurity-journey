Strong Passwords
  Use long, unique passwords for every account
  Length is more important than complexity
  Avoid:
    Reusing passwords
    Personal info (birthdays, names)
    Common patterns (123456, password)

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Adds a second layer of security:
  Something you know → password
  Something you have → phone/app
  Something you are → biometrics

Even if password is stolen, attacker is blocked

Types of MFA:
  SMS codes (least secure)
  Authenticator apps (recommended)
  Hardware keys (most secure)
